The Planets

Mercury

Mercury is the first planet from the sun. In the day, the planet is scorching hot with temperatures getting up to 354 degrees Fahrenheit.
‍
At night, the planet drops down to -330 degrees Fahrenheit making life difficult to survive. The planet rotates at a slow pace (59 Earth days to rotate once) but its orbit is much faster (88 Earth Days in a year).

Quick Facts:
‍

  • Diameter: 4,879.4 km
  • Distance from Sun: 57,909,175 km
  • Type: Rocky Planet
  • Moon: 0
  • Average temp: 354 degrees F

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the sun. The planet has a thick atmosphere (made mostly of Carbon Dioxide) that traps in heat which makes the planet a sweltering 847 degrees Fahrenheit.
‍
A few Russian spacecrafts tried to land on this planet, but it was quickly melted by the heat and crushed by the heavy atmosphere. Luckily, they were able to send back pictures of the surface of the planet before they met their fate.
‍
There is still much to learn about this planet and what is under the thick layers of clouds.
‍
Quick Facts:

  • Diameter: 12,104 km
  • Distance from Sun: 108,208,930 km
  • Type: Rocky Planet
  • Moon: 0
  • Average temp:  847 degrees F

Earth

The Earth is the third planet in the sun. Our planet is in the “Goldilock Zone” which means we are the perfect distance away from the sun where water does not evaporate or freeze. 70% of our planet is covered in water.
‍
The Earth is very much alive with an active molten core that moves around. Without this movement from the core, we would not have a magnetic field that protects us from the Sun’s radiation.

Quick Facts:
‍

  • Diameter: 12,756km
  • Distance from Sun: 149,597,890 km
  • Type: Rocky Planet
  • Moon: 1
  • Average temp:  61 degrees F

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the sun. This planet has long been a fascination to humans where we sent a rover to explore the little, dusty, red planet. The longest lasting rover that explored the planet was Opportunity which lasted for 14 years. This little rover helped us discover many secrets from this mysterious planet.
‍
It was also believed that Mars once harbored life atone point. But with no magnetic field to protect the planets surface, the Sun’s radiation stripped the atmosphere and any potential life.

Quick Facts:

  • Diameter: 6,794km
  • Distance from Sun: 227,936,640km
  • Type: Rocky Planet
  • Moon: 2
  • Average temp:  -81 degrees F

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the sun. Jupiter is different from all the other planets in the solar system because it is the largest planet and it has no definitive surface.
‍
Jupiter is mostly made up of hydrogen and helium, is considered a gas giant, and is speculated to have been a star if it was large enough to commence thermonuclear reactions.

Quick Facts:

  • Diameter: 142,984 km
  • Distance from Sun: 778,412,020 km
  • Type: Gas Giant
  • Moons: 63
  • Average temp: -238 degrees F

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the sun. Saturn is the second largest planet in our solar system and has an average temperature of about -285ºF.
‍
Saturn gets its famous rings from pieces of comets, asteroids or shattered moons that broke up before they reached the planet, torn apart by Saturn's powerful gravity. Saturn's ring system extends up to 282,000 kilometers from the planet.

Quick Facts:

  • Diameter: 120,530 km
  • Distance from Sun: 1,426,725,400 km
  • Type: Gas Giant
  • Moons: 35
  • Average temp: -285 degrees F

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the sun. Uranus is unique from all others planets in the solar system because it has vastly different poles than all the other planets.
‍
Uranus’s vertical equator likely comes from a hit from a large enough object to knock it off of it’s original rotation. Uranus’s average temperature is -353ºF.

Quick Facts:

  • Diameter: 51,118 km
  • Distance from Sun: 2,870,972,200 km
  • Type: Gas Giant
  • Moons: 27
  • Average temp: -353 degrees F

Neptune

Neptune is the blank planet from the sun. Neptune’s winds are the fastest in our solar system and Neptune’s also the coldest planet in our solar system.
‍
Neptune also has a very thin ring structure that cannot be seen from earth, but was discovered and photographed by Voyager.
‍
Quick Facts:

  • Diameter: 49,528 km
  • Distance from Sun: 4,498,252,900 km
  • Type: Gas Giant
  • Moons: 13
  • Average temp: -392 degrees F

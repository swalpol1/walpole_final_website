The Sun

Quick Facts About The Sun

  • Diameter: 1,390,000 km
  • Core temp: 15,600,000 Degrees
  • Age: about 4.5 billion years
  • Life-span: 9-10 billion years
  • Star type: average yellow star

The sun is the most important thing in our solar system to life itself. Earth depends on the sun for life. It helps grow food, keeps the planet from being ice, and it’s where we came from. All the planets and life as we know it is made from left over star dust. The sun also keeps the Earth from flying away into deep dark space. The gravity from the sun keeps the planets ,comets, and asteroids orbiting around it.

The Sun's Heat

As mentioned earlier, the sun keeps the Earth warm. If we were too far from the Sun, the waters would freeze, and life would be hard to exists. If we were any closer to the sun, our waters would evaporate and Earth would be a dry desert. How does the sun produce this heat? The Sun produces this through a process called nuclear fusion which happens in the Sun’s core. This energy travels up towards the surface (photosphere) and the heat is radiated throughout the Solar System. This fusion gives us life as we know it.

Dancing Lights

Up in the North and South Poles, there are beautiful dancing lights which is caused from our very own Sun. How does this happen? The Sun has a layer of gas called corona and this is very hot. Because this layer is so hot, the Sun cannot contain it and it releases it at one million miles per hour. This gas that is released is called plasma. This plasma hits the Earth’s magnetosphere and follows the field lines into the polars (North & South poles). When this happens, the plasma hits the nitrogen and oxygen molecules which creates the beautiful lights we know as the Northern & Southern lights or another name, Auroras.

Sun Spots

When looking on the sun, there are dark specs all over the surface. These are called sunspots. These spots are created when there is a massive blast of magnetic energy (Solar Flare). After this happens, the spot where it was released is a lot cooler than the surface of the sun. With these Solar flares, they end up hitting Earth which most of the times we do not notice. However, it is possible for there to be a massive solar flare that hits Earth and knocks out most of our technology and electricity.

The Moon

Quick Facts About The Moon

  • Diameter: 2158.8 mi
  • Distance to Earth: 238,900 mi
  • Age: 4.53 billion years
  • Gravity: 1.62 m/s²

The moon has been earth’s closest neighbor for approximately 4.53 billion years. The moon is earth’s only natural satellite and is the most visible object seen in our night sky. The moon affects our oceans tides and its orbit coincides with the end of our 12 hours of daylight.

Moon Landing

The famous moon landing took place on July 16, 1969. Apollo 11 launched from Cape Kennedy carrying Commander Neil Armstrong, Command Module Pilot Michael Collins and Lunar Module Pilot Edwin "Buzz" Aldrin into an initial Earth-orbit of 114 by 116 miles. It took the crew around 76 hours to reach the moon and broadcasted the occurrence to roughly 650 million people as Neil Armstrong took the first steps on the moon.

Moon Phases

The eight phases of the moon take place from the varying amounts of illumination on the moon during its orbit around the earth.

Lunar and Solar Eclipse

During a lunar eclipse, Earth comes between the Sun and the Moon, blocking the sunlight falling on the Moon. Solar eclipses occur when the moon gets in between the sun and the earth and blocks the light of the sun.
